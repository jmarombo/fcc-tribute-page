Dr. Henry Norman Bethune

Canadian Surgeon and Communist

Here's a timeline of Dr. Bethune's life

  • 1980 - Born in Gravenhurst, Ontario
  • 1907 - Graduated from Owen Sound Collegiate and Vocational Institute (OSCVI); joined University of Toronto
  • 1914 - Abandoned his medical studies when the First World War was declared in Europe.
  • 1916 - Went back to Toronto to complete his medical education; received his M.D.
  • 1920's - Underwent medical studies in London and Edinburgh.
  • 1922- Elected as a Fellow of the Royal College of Surgeons.
  • 1923 - Married Frances Penny; divorced when he develops Tuberculosis.
  • 1924 - Opened a private medical practice in Detroit, Michigan.
  • 1928 - Served as the first assistant of Dr. Edward Archibald, the Canadian innovator in thoracic surgery in Montreal, Quebec at McGill University.
  • 1929 - Married to Frances again.
  • 1929 - 1936 - Improved skills in the field of thoracic surgery; developed several new surgical tools.
  • 1933 - Divorced again and forever.
  • 1935 - Attended International Physiological Congress in Moscow.
  • 1936 - Joined the Communist Party.
  • 1936 - Set up a mobile blood transfusion service in November; Provides services to the Spanish Republican Government.
  • 1938 - Went to China as medical chief of the Red Army.
  • 1939 - Bethune lost his life on 12 November, while fighting for the Chinese.
